Gulab Jamun

Soft and spongy milk solids dumplings soaked in sugar syrup.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes


Makes: 20 pieces

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or unsalted butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup milk, or as needed to knead the dough
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
  2. Add melted ghee or butter and mix well.
  3. Add milk gradually and knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  4. In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, crushed cardamom pods, and saffron strands (if using). Bring to a boil and simmer for 5-7 minutes to make sugar syrup. Keep it warm.
  5. Divide the dough into small equal-sized portions and shape them into smooth balls without any cracks.
  6. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, reduce the heat to low and carefully add the dough balls one by one.
  7. Fry the balls on low heat, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown and evenly cooked from all sides.
  8. Remove the fried balls using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  9. Transfer the fried balls into the warm sugar syrup and let them soak for at least 2-3 hours before serving.
  10. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.

Pineapple Kichadi

A refreshing South Indian side dish made with pineapple, yogurt, and coconut.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 25 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chopped pineapple
  • 1 cup yogurt (preferably sour)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, whisk the yogurt until smooth. Add chopped pineapple and grated coconut. Mix well and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, chopped green chili, and curry leaves. Saut√© for a minute.
  4. Pour this tempering over the yogurt-pineapple mixture. Mix gently to combine.
  5. Add salt to taste and mix well.
  6. Refrigerate for 1-2 hours before serving to enhance the flavors.
  7. Serve chilled as a side dish with rice or enjoy as a refreshing snack.

Rasmalai

Soft, spongy cottage cheese dumplings soaked in sweetened, thickened milk flavored with cardamom and saffron.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes

Makes: 10 pieces

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • A few saffron strands
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Boil the milk in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Once it comes to a boil, reduce the heat and add lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring continuously until the milk curdles.
  2. Once the milk curdles completely, switch off the heat and let it rest for a few minutes.
  3. Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth or a fine mesh strainer. Rinse the chenna (strained milk solids) under cold water to remove the lemony flavor.
  4. Squeeze out excess water from the chenna and hang it for about 30 minutes to drain excess moisture.
  5. Transfer the drained chenna to a plate and knead it for 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and soft. Divide the dough into small equal-sized portions and shape them into smooth balls without any cracks.
  6. In a wide pan, combine sugar, water, crushed cardamom pods, and saffron strands. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5-7 minutes to make sugar syrup.
  7. Gently drop the chenna balls into the simmering syrup. Cover and cook for about 15-20 minutes until the balls double in size and become spongy.
  8. Remove the cooked balls from the syrup and let them cool slightly.
  9. Transfer the balls to a serving dish and pour the thickened, sweetened milk over them.
  10. Chill the rasmalai for a few hours before serving. Garnish with chopped nuts before serving.

Mango Lassi

A refreshing and creamy yogurt-based drink flavored with sweet mango pulp.

Preparation Time: 5 minutes | Total Time: 5 minutes

Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ripe mango pulp
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/2 cup cold milk
  • 2-3 tablespoons sugar or honey (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom (optional)
  • A few ice cubes (optional)
  • Chopped pistachios or mint leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine mango pulp, plain yogurt, cold milk, sugar or honey, and ground cardamom.
  2. Blend until smooth and creamy.
  3. If desired, add a few ice cubes and blend again until frothy.
  4. Pour the mango lassi into serving glasses.
  5. Garnish with chopped pistachios or mint leaves.
  6. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Rasgulla

Soft and spongy cottage cheese balls cooked in sugar syrup.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes

Makes: 12 pieces

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 cardamom pods, crushed
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pan, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat and add lemon juice or vinegar gradually while stirring continuously until the milk curdles.
  3. Switch off the heat and let it rest for a few minutes.
  4. Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth or a fine mesh strainer. Rinse the chenna (strained milk solids) under cold water to remove the lemony flavor.
  5. Squeeze out excess water from the chenna and hang it for about 30 minutes to drain excess moisture.
  6. Transfer the drained chenna to a plate and knead it for 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and soft.
  7. Divide the dough into small equal-sized portions and shape them into smooth balls without any cracks.
  8. In a separate pot, combine sugar, water, crushed cardamom pods, and saffron strands (if using). Bring to a boil to make sugar syrup.
  9. Gently drop the chenna balls into the boiling syrup. Cover and cook for about 15-20 minutes until the balls double in size and become spongy.
  10. Remove the cooked rasgullas from the syrup and let them cool down.
  11. Refrigerate for a few hours before serving. Serve chilled.

Kulfi

Creamy and rich Indian ice cream flavored with saffron, cardamom, and nuts.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Makes: 6 servings

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1/4 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (pistachios, almonds)

Instructions:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the milk starts boiling, reduce the heat and add sweetened condensed milk, milk powder, and sugar. Mix well.
  3. Simmer the mixture on low heat, stirring continuously, until it thickens and reduces to half its original volume.
  4. Add cardamom powder, saffron strands, and chopped nuts. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Remove the pan from heat and let the mixture cool down to room temperature.
  6. Pour the mixture into kulfi molds or small serving cups. Insert wooden sticks into the center of each kulfi.
  7. Freeze the kulfi molds or cups for at least 6-8 hours or overnight until they are set completely.
  8. To unmold the kulfi, dip the molds briefly in warm water and gently pull out the kulfi sticks.
  9. Serve the kulfi immediately. Enjoy!

Jalebi

Crispy and syrupy Indian sweet made from fermented flour batter.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes


Makes: 20 pieces

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon gram flour (besan)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands (optional)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, gram flour, yogurt, baking soda, and turmeric powder.
  2. Gradually add water and mix to form a smooth, lump-free batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter.
  3. Cover the batter and let it ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  4. In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Bring to a boil and simmer for 8-10 minutes to make sugar syrup. Keep it warm.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  6. Transfer the fermented batter to a squeeze bottle or a piping bag with a small nozzle.
  7. Squeeze the batter into hot oil in circular motions to form spirals or pretzel shapes.
  8. Fry the jalebis until they are crisp and golden brown on both sides.
  9. Remove the fried jalebis using a slotted spoon and immediately dip them into the warm sugar syrup.
  10. Let the jalebis soak in the syrup for a few seconds, then remove and place them on a serving plate.
  11. Serve the jalebis warm or at room temperature. Enjoy!

Shahi Tukda

Rich and indulgent bread pudding soaked in flavored sugar syrup and topped with nuts.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of bread
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2-3 cardamom pods, crushed
  • A few saffron strands
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cut the bread slices into triangles or squares.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan and lightly fry the bread slices until golden brown on both sides. Drain excess ghee and set aside.
  3. In another pan, bring whole milk to a boil. Add crushed cardamom pods and saffron strands.
  4. Reduce the heat and simmer the milk until it reduces to half its original quantity, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add sugar to the milk and stir until it dissolves completely.
  6. Place the fried bread slices in a serving dish and pour the reduced milk over them, ensuring all slices are soaked well.
  7. Let the shahi tukda rest for at least 30 minutes to allow the bread to absorb the milk.
  8. Garnish with chopped nuts before serving. Serve warm or chilled, as desired.

Rabri

Creamy and rich Indian dessert made by reducing milk with sugar and flavored with cardamom and saffron.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Total Time: 1 hour 10 minutes

Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A few saffron strands
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a wide heavy-bottomed pan, bring the whole milk to a boil over medium heat.
  2. Once the milk comes to a boil, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer.
  3. Keep stirring the milk at regular intervals and scrape the cream collected on the sides of the pan.
  4. After simmering for about 30 minutes, add sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron strands to the milk.
  5. Continue to simmer the milk until it reduces to about one-third of its original volume and becomes thick and creamy.
  6. Turn off the heat and let the rabri cool down to room temperature.
  7. Transfer the rabri to a serving bowl and garnish with chopped nuts.
  8. Serve chilled or at room temperature. Enjoy the rich and creamy rabri!
