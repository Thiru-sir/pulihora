Masala Dosa

A popular South Indian dish consisting of a thin, crispy pancake filled with a spicy potato filling.

Prep Time: 8 hours | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 8 hours 30 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups dosa rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak dosa rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds together for 6-8 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter using a wet grinder or blender. Add salt and mix well. Ferment the batter overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  3. For the potato filling, heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  5. Add turmeric powder and asafoetida. Mix well.
  6. Add mashed potatoes and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat and add chopped coriander leaves. Mix well and set aside.
  8. To make dosas, heat a non-stick skillet or dosa tawa. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  9. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crisp.
  10. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the dosa and fold it over. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Neer Dosa

Neer dosa is a traditional South Indian crepe made from rice batter. It is light, soft, and pairs well with various chutneys and curries.

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 25 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • Water (as needed)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 4-5 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice and transfer it to a blender. Add a little water and blend until you get a smooth batter with pouring consistency. The batter should be thinner than regular dosa batter.
  3. Add salt to the batter and mix well.
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet or dosa tawa over medium heat.
  5. Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the skillet and quickly spread it in a circular motion to form a thin dosa.
  6. Drizzle some oil around the edges of the dosa and cook until the edges start to lift and the surface of the dosa appears cooked.
  7. Using a spatula, gently loosen the edges and fold the dosa into half or roll it if desired.
  8. Repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any curry of your choice.

Kerala Beef Fry

A delicious and spicy Kerala-style beef fry, perfect as a side dish or appetizer.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes




Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add thinly sliced onions, green chilies, and curry leaves. Saute until onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add thinly sliced beef and salt. Cook until the beef is browned.
  7. Add grated coconut and mix well. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until the beef is well coated with the masala and the coconut is toasted.
  8. Adjust seasoning according to taste.
  9. Once done, garnish with curry leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Goli Baje (Mangalore Bajji)

Goli Baje, also known as Mangalore Bajji, is a popular South Indian snack made from a fermented batter of lentils and spices.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes | Fermentation Time: 3-4 hours | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 4 hours 25 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak urad dal in water for 3-4 hours. Drain well.
  2. Grind the soaked urad dal into a smooth batter using minimal water.
  3. Transfer the batter into a bowl and add rice flour, grated ginger, chopped green chilies, cumin seeds, asafoetida, chopped coriander leaves, and salt. Mix well to combine.
  4. Allow the batter to ferment for 3-4 hours or until it becomes slightly fluffy.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  6. Scoop small portions of the batter using a spoon and gently drop them into the hot oil.
  7. Fry the goli bajje in batches until they turn golden brown and crisp.
  8. Remove from oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Udupi Sambar

Udupi Sambar is a delicious South Indian lentil-based vegetable stew made with a medley of vegetables, tamarind, and aromatic spices.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 1 medium-sized potato, peeled and cubed
  • 1 small carrot, peeled and chopped
  • 5-6 green beans, chopped
  • 1 small tomato, chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped bottle gourd (lauki)
  • 1/4 cup chopped pumpkin
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 2 tablespoons grated coconut
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook the toor dal until soft and mushy. Mash and set aside.
  2. Soak the tamarind in warm water for 15-20 minutes. Extract the juice and set aside.
  3. In a large pot, heat oil and add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add fenugreek seeds, dry red chilies, and asafoetida.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  5. Add all the chopped vegetables, slit green chilies, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Add tamarind extract and bring the mixture to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer until vegetables are cooked through.
  7. Add sambar powder and mashed toor dal. Mix well and simmer for another 5-7 minutes.
  8. In a small pan, heat a teaspoon of oil and roast grated coconut until golden brown. Add it to the sambar and mix well.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or idli.

Malabar Parotta

Malabar Parotta, also known as Kerala Parotta, is a popular South Indian flatbread made from maida flour. It is flaky, crispy, and goes well with a variety of side dishes.

Preparation Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 1 hour

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Water as needed
  • Ghee or oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, sugar, salt, and ghee or oil.
  2. Add water gradually and knead into a soft, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll each ball into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin and some oil.
  4. Brush the rolled dough disc with ghee or oil and make pleats by folding it like a paper fan.
  5. Roll the pleated dough into a tight spiral and tuck the end underneath. Repeat with the remaining dough balls.
  6. Flatten each spiral gently with your palm and roll into a thin, round parotta.
  7. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat and place the rolled parotta on it.
  8. Cook until brown spots appear on one side, then flip and cook the other side until golden brown, adding ghee or oil as needed.
  9. Repeat with the remaining dough balls.
  10. Serve hot with your favorite curry or side dish.

Kothu Parotta

Kothu Parotta is a popular South Indian street food made with shredded parotta, mixed with a spicy masala, vegetables, and sometimes eggs or meat.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4 leftover parottas, shredded
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 capsicum, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan or kadhai. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  2. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  3. If using eggs, push the onion-tomato mixture to one side of the pan and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until cooked.
  4. Add chopped capsicum and cook for a couple of minutes.
  5. Add shredded parottas and mix well with the masala.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix until everything is well combined.
  7. Continue to cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the parottas are heated through and well coated with the masala.
  8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot.

Kerala Fish Moilee

Kerala Fish Moilee is a traditional fish curry from Kerala, India, made with coconut milk, spices, and fish fillets. It has a creamy texture and subtle flavors, making it a delightful dish for seafood lovers.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500g fish fillets (such as kingfish or tilapia)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1 onion, finely sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, julienned
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tomato, sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Clean and cut the fish fillets into pieces. Marinate them with turmeric powder and salt. Keep aside for 10 minutes.
  2. Heat coconut oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add sliced onions, green chilies, ginger, and garlic. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add curry leaves, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add sliced tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  6. Now, add marinated fish pieces and mix gently with the masala.
  7. Pour thick coconut milk and adjust salt. Bring it to a gentle simmer. Do not boil vigorously as it may cause the coconut milk to curdle.
  8. Cover and cook for 8-10 minutes or until the fish is cooked through.
  9. Once done, remove from heat and garnish with fresh curry leaves.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice or appam.

Puliyotharai (Tamarind Rice)

Puliyotharai is a tangy and spicy South Indian rice dish made with tamarind pulp, spices, and rice. It is a popular recipe prepared during festivals and special occasions.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1 lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice in a pot or rice cooker and let it cool.
  2. Soak tamarind in warm water for 15 minutes. Extract the pulp and discard the seeds.
  3. Heat sesame oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add peanuts and roast until golden brown. Remove and keep aside.
  5. In the same pan, add chana dal, urad dal, fenugreek seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the dals turn golden brown.
  6. Add turmeric powder and sauté for a few seconds.
  7. Add the tamarind pulp, jaggery (if using), and salt. Cook until the mixture thickens.
  8. Once the mixture thickens, add the cooked rice and roasted peanuts. Mix well until the rice is coated evenly with the tamarind mixture.
  9. Remove from heat and serve hot or at room temperature.
  10. Puliyotharai is ready to be served. Enjoy!

Rasam

Rasam is a traditional South Indian soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, spices, and herbs. It is served as a side dish with rice or enjoyed as a soup.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak tamarind in warm water for 15 minutes. Extract the pulp and discard the seeds.
  2. In a pot, add chopped tomatoes, tamarind pulp, turmeric powder, and salt. Add water and bring it to a boil.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add cumin seeds, dry red chilies, curry leaves, asafoetida, and crushed garlic. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add this tempering to the boiling rasam mixture.
  6. Let the rasam simmer for 5-7 minutes until the flavors are well combined.
  7. Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  8. Rasam is ready to be served hot with rice or enjoyed as a soup!

Paal Payasam

Paal Payasam, also known as Rice Kheer, is a traditional South Indian dessert made with rice, milk, sugar, and flavored with cardamom and saffron.

Preparation Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes


Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 1 liter whole milk
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (cashews, almonds, pistachios)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil.
  3. Add the soaked rice to the boiling milk and cook on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice is soft and the milk thickens (about 30-40 minutes).
  4. Add sugar and continue to cook until the sugar dissolves completely.
  5. Crush the saffron strands and dissolve them in a tablespoon of warm milk. Add this saffron milk and cardamom powder to the payasam and mix well.
  6. Heat ghee in a small pan and fry the chopped nuts until golden brown. Add these fried nuts to the payasam.
  7. Simmer for a few more minutes until the payasam reaches the desired consistency.
  8. Turn off the heat and let the payasam cool slightly before serving.
  9. Serve warm or chilled, garnished with more chopped nuts if desired.

Chicken 65

Chicken 65 is a popular spicy, deep-fried chicken dish originating from South India. It is made with boneless chicken marinated in a flavorful blend of spices and deep-fried until crispy.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon corn flour
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 egg
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Curry leaves and green chilies for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, corn flour, all-purpose flour, yogurt, egg, and salt to make a smooth marinade.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and mix well. Allow it to marinate for at least 1 hour in the refrigerator.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Once the oil is hot, carefully add the marinated chicken pieces one by one into the oil and fry until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  5. Remove the fried chicken from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  6. Garnish with curry leaves and slit green chilies.
  7. Serve hot as an appetizer or as a side dish with rice or roti.
