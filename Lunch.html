Lemon Rice

A crepe-like dish made from green gram (moong dal) and rice batter, typically served with ginger chutney or upma.

Prep Time: 6-8 hours | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 8 hours 15 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice (preferably basmati)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Juice of 1-2 lemons (adjust to taste)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice according to package instructions and allow it to cool slightly.
  2. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Next, add urad dal and chana dal. Sauté until dals turn golden brown.
  5. Add asafoetida, slit green chilies, peanuts, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Lower the heat and add turmeric powder and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add cooked rice to the pan and gently mix everything together until rice is well-coated with the tempering.
  8. Turn off the heat and squeeze lemon juice over the rice. Mix well.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves (if using).
  10. Serve hot or at room temperature.

Curry leaves rice

A spicy and aromatic South Indian chicken curry flavored with traditional Chettinad spices.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup fresh curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Then, drain the water and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat.
  3. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add urad dal and chana dal. Sauté until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add chopped green chilies and fresh curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Add the soaked and drained basmati rice to the pan. Stir well to coat the rice with the tempering.
  7. Pour in 2 cups of water and add salt to taste. Mix well.
  8. Cover the pan with a lid and cook the rice on low heat until it's fully cooked and fluffy.
  9. If using grated coconut, sprinkle it over the cooked rice and gently fluff the rice with a fork.
  10. Remove from heat and let it sit for a few minutes before serving.
  11. Serve hot as a main dish or as a side with yogurt or pickle.

Coconut Rice

A flavorful South Indian rice dish made with fresh coconut, typically served with a side of papad or pickle.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 1 cup freshly grated coconut
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add urad dal, chana dal, and dried red chilies. Sauté until dals turn golden brown.
  5. Add curry leaves and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Add turmeric powder and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add grated coconut to the pan and sauté for 2-3 minutes until it turns aromatic.
  8. Add rinsed rice to the pan and mix well with the tempering and coconut.
  9. Add water and bring it to a boil. Then, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until the rice is cooked and water is absorbed.
  10. Once done, fluff the rice with a fork.
  11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot.

Tomato Rice

A flavorful rice dish made with tomatoes, spices, and cooked rice, perfect for a quick and delicious meal.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and slit green chilies. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy and oil starts to separate.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add cooked rice to the pan and gently mix everything together until rice is well-coated with the tomato mixture.
  8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  9. Serve hot with raita or yogurt.

Curd Rice

A comforting and cooling dish made from cooked rice mixed with yogurt and seasoned with tempering, typically served as a main course or side dish.

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Mash the cooked rice using the back of a spoon or a potato masher until it becomes slightly mushy.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the plain yogurt until smooth.
  3. Mix the mashed rice with the whisked yogurt until well combined. Add salt to taste and mix again.
  4. Heat oil or ghee in a small pan over medium heat.
  5. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  6. Next, add urad dal and chana dal. Sauté until dals turn golden brown.
  7. Add chopped green chilies, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
  8. Pour the tempering over the curd rice mixture and mix well.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.

Parupu Rasam

A comforting South Indian soup made with lentils, tomatoes, tamarind, and a blend of spices, often served with steamed rice.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 medium-sized tomato, chopped
  • 1 small lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, crushed (optional)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil

Instructions:

  1. Cook toor dal in a pressure cooker until soft and mushy. Set aside.
  2. Soak tamarind in warm water for about 10 minutes. Extract tamarind juice and discard the pulp.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  6. Add turmeric powder, asafoetida, crushed garlic (if using), and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add tamarind extract and bring it to a boil. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  8. Add cooked toor dal and mix well. Add water to adjust the consistency if needed.
  9. Boil the rasam for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice.

Egg Rice

A flavorful rice dish made with eggs, spices, and vegetables, perfect for a quick and satisfying meal.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  5. Break the eggs into the pan and scramble them with the vegetables.
  6. Add turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add cooked rice to the pan and mix everything together until well-combined.
  8. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the rice is heated through.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Fried Rice

A flavorful dish made from cooked rice stir-fried with vegetables, eggs, and soy sauce.

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, etc.), diced
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add beaten eggs and scramble until cooked. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Heat the remaining oil in the same pan. Add minced garlic and diced onion. Sauté until fragrant and onions turn translucent.
  4. Add diced vegetables to the pan and stir-fry for a few minutes until they are tender but still crisp.
  5. Add cooked rice to the pan and stir-fry with the vegetables.
  6. Pour soy sauce and oyster sauce (if using) over the rice. Mix well to coat evenly.
  7. Add the scrambled eggs back to the pan. Stir-fry everything together until heated through.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.
  10. Serve hot as a delicious main course or side dish.

Vermicelli Upma

A savory South Indian breakfast dish made from vermicelli noodles, tempered with spices, and mixed with vegetables.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 25 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups vermicelli noodles
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped carrots
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green beans
  • 1/4 cup frozen peas
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add urad dal and chana dal. Sauté until dals turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  5. Add chopped carrots, green beans, peas, and green chilies. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add asafoetida, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Break the vermicelli noodles into smaller pieces and add them to the pan. Roast the vermicelli for 2-3 minutes until lightly golden.
  8. Pour 2 cups of water into the pan and mix well. Cover and cook for 5-7 minutes or until the vermicelli is cooked and water is absorbed.
  9. Once done, fluff up the vermicelli upma with a fork.
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves (if using).
  11. Serve hot as a breakfast or snack.

Aloo Gobi

A flavorful Indian curry made with potatoes and cauliflower, spiced with aromatic Indian spices.

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 small cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  6. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add diced potatoes and cauliflower florets. Mix until the vegetables are coated with the spices.
  8. Cover and cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes and cauliflower are tender.
  9. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.

Sambar Sadam

A flavorful one-pot meal made by cooking rice with mixed vegetables and sambar powder, typically served with papad or raita.

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1/4 cup green beans, chopped
  • 1/4 cup peas
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Wash rice and toor dal together, then soak in water for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pressure cooker.
  3. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until mustard seeds crackle.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  6. Add chopped vegetables, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Drain water from soaked rice and dal, then add them to the cooker. Mix everything together.
  8. Add 4 cups of water and close the pressure cooker lid. Cook for 3-4 whistles or until rice and dal are cooked well.
  9. Once the pressure releases, open the cooker and give the sambar sadam a gentle mix.
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with papad or raita.

Rasam

A tangy and spicy South Indian soup made with tamarind, tomatoes, and a blend of spices, typically served with steamed rice or as a soup.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 small lemon-sized ball of tamarind
  • 3 ripe tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak tamarind in warm water for 10-15 minutes. Extract the tamarind juice and discard the pulp.
  2. In a deep pan or pot, heat oil over medium heat.
  3. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, dried red chilies, and asafoetida. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, slit green chilies, curry leaves, and crushed garlic (if using). Cook until tomatoes are soft and mushy.
  6. Add tamarind juice and salt to taste. Bring the rasam to a boil.
  7. Simmer the rasam for 5-7 minutes on low heat. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  8. Turn off the heat and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice or as a soup.
