Paya Kari

A rich and flavorful South Indian curry made with tender goat trotters.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 2 hours | Total Time: 2 hours 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 4-6 goat trotters (paya)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Clean and wash the goat trotters thoroughly. Pressure cook them until tender with some salt and turmeric powder.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  6. Add coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add the cooked goat trotters along with the broth. Mix well and simmer for about 30-40 minutes until the flavors meld together.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Mutton Kola Urundai

Deep-fried meatballs made with minced mutton and aromatic spices.

Preparation Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams minced mutton
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon garam masala
  • A handful of coriander leaves, chopped
  • A handful of mint leaves, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine minced mutton, chopped onions, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, fennel seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, chopped coriander leaves, chopped mint leaves, beaten egg, gram flour, and salt. Mix well to form a dough-like consistency.
  2. Take small portions of the mixture and shape them into small round balls (urundai).
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once the oil is hot, deep fry the mutton balls in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  4. Remove from oil and drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  5. Serve hot with your favorite sauce or chutney.

Monica Chicken

A spicy and tangy chicken curry with a unique blend of flavors.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup tamarind extract
  • 1 tablespoon jaggery or brown sugar
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, slit green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add chicken pieces and sauté until they are well coated with the masala.
  7. Add tamarind extract and jaggery. Mix well and bring it to a boil.
  8. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 20-25 minutes until the chicken is cooked through and the gravy thickens.
  9. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Kari Dosai

A delicious South Indian dish consisting of dosa served with a spicy mutton curry.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • Dosai batter (as required)
  • 500 grams mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add mutton pieces, turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add coconut milk and enough water to cover the mutton pieces. Pressure cook for 4-5 whistles or until the mutton is tender.
  7. Once the pressure releases, open the cooker and cook on high flame until the gravy thickens.
  8. To make dosai, heat a tawa and pour a ladleful of dosa batter. Spread it in a circular motion.
  9. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden brown.
  10. Serve hot dosai with the prepared mutton curry.

Mutton Soup

A comforting and nutritious soup made with tender mutton pieces and flavored with aromatic spices.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 45 minutes | Total Time: 1 hour

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams mutton pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2 cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pressure cooker. Add cumin seeds, peppercorns, cinnamon stick, cloves, cardamom pods, and bay leaf. Sauté for a minute.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add mutton pieces, salt, and enough water to cover the mutton. Pressure cook for 5-6 whistles or until the mutton is tender.
  6. Once the pressure releases, open the cooker and add curry leaves and chopped coriander leaves.
  7. Simmer for 5-10 minutes on low heat. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.
  8. Serve hot mutton soup garnished with coriander leaves.

Thalapakatti Biryani

A flavorful and aromatic biryani originating from the region of Dindigul, Tamil Nadu, India.

Preparation Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Total Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500 grams mutton, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4-5 whole cloves
  • 4-5 whole black peppercorns
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, peppercorns, and cardamom pods. Sauté for a minute.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  5. Add mutton pieces and cook until they are browned.
  6. Add yogurt, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Cover and cook until the mutton is tender.
  8. Meanwhile, boil water in a separate pot. Add soaked basmati rice and cook until 70-80% done. Drain the excess water.
  9. Add the partially cooked rice to the mutton mixture. Cover and cook on low heat for 10-15 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors are well absorbed.
  10. If using saffron, dissolve it in a tablespoon of warm milk and drizzle over the biryani. Garnish with coriander leaves before serving.

Fish Gravy

A delicious South Indian curry made with fish pieces cooked in a spicy and tangy gravy.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams fish pieces (any firm fish like kingfish, tilapia, or salmon)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup tamarind extract
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add tamarind extract and enough water to make a gravy consistency.
  7. Bring the gravy to a boil and then simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  8. Add fish pieces and curry leaves. Cover and cook until the fish is cooked through.
  9. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or dosa.

Crab Fry

A delicious and spicy South Indian crab fry recipe.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams crab, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, asafoetida, curry leaves, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add crab pieces and mix well to coat them with the masala.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes until the crab is cooked through.
  8. Once the crab is cooked and the masala is dry, garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.

Muttai Masala

A spicy and flavorful South Indian egg curry.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 35 minutes



Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 6 eggs, hard-boiled and peeled
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, curry leaves, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add coconut milk and bring the gravy to a gentle simmer.
  7. Cut the boiled eggs in half lengthwise and add them to the gravy.
  8. Cover and cook for a few minutes until the eggs are heated through and the gravy thickens.
  9. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Kari Kolambu

A spicy and tangy South Indian curry made with mutton.

Preparation Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 1 hour | Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams mutton pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 dry red chilies
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 cup tamarind extract
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pressure cooker. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add mutton pieces and sauté until they are well coated with the masala.
  7. Add tamarind extract and enough water to cover the mutton pieces.
  8. Cover the pressure cooker and cook for about 4-5 whistles or until the mutton is tender.
  9. Once the pressure releases, open the cooker, check the consistency, and adjust salt if needed.
  10. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.

Chilli Mutton Fry

A spicy and fiery dry mutton dish, perfect as a starter or side.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams mutton, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  4. Add slit green chilies, black pepper powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  5. Add mutton pieces and sauté until they are well coated with the masala.
  6. Cover and cook on low heat until the mutton is tender and cooked through.
  7. Add soy sauce and curry leaves. Mix well and cook for another 5 minutes.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot as a starter or side dish.

Mutton Chukka

A dry and spicy South Indian mutton dish, packed with flavors.

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams mutton pieces
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon black pepper corns, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon cumin seeds

  • 1 tablespoon garam masala

Instructions:
