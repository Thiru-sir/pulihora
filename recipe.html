Recipes & Cooking Ideas

From the fragrant spices of Tamil Nadu to the coconut-infused dishes of Kerala and the fiery delicacies of Andhra Pradesh, discover the rich flavors of South India.

Dosa

A popular South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter.

Prep Time: 8 hours | Cook Time: 10 minutes | Total Time: 8 hours 10 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately in water for 6-8 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients into a smooth batter. Add salt and mix well.
  3. Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle. Pour a ladleful of batter and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  4. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden brown and crisp.
  5. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Sambhar

A flavorful South Indian lentil stew made with vegetables and spices.

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 45 minutes

Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, potatoes, drumsticks, beans)
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook toor dal with water until soft and mushy. Mash and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add curry leaves, chopped onions, and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  4. Add mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, sambar powder, tamarind paste, and salt. Mix well and cook until vegetables are tender.
  5. Add cooked toor dal and water to adjust the consistency. Bring it to a boil and simmer for a few minutes.
  6. Prepare tempering by heating oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds and curry leaves. Once they splutter, pour the tempering over the sambhar.
  7. Serve hot with idli, dosa, or rice.

Vegetable Biryani

A flavorful rice dish cooked with mixed vegetables and aromatic spices.

Preparation Time: 30 mins | Cook Time: 30 minutes |

Serves: 6

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, potatoes, beans)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 3 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee/oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add sliced onions, ginger, garlic, and green chili. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add mixed vegetables, turmeric powder, garam masala, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add soaked rice, yogurt, salt, and water. Mix well.
  7. Cover and cook on low heat until rice is cooked and all the water is absorbed.
  8. Fluff the rice gently with a fork and serve hot.

Hyderabadi Biryani

A classic Indian dish made with flavorful chicken and aromatic spices.

Preparation Time: 30 mins | Cook Time: 40 minutes |


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 3 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat ghee/oil in a large pot. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add sliced onions, ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté until onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add chicken pieces and cook until they are no longer pink.
  6. Add turmeric powder, garam masala, mint leaves, coriander leaves, yogurt, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add soaked rice and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and cover. Cook for 20 minutes or until rice is tender and chicken is cooked through.
  8. Fluff the rice gently with a fork and serve hot.

Puttu Kadala Curry

A traditional Kerala breakfast consisting of steamed rice and coconut cylinders served with spicy chickpea curry.

Preparation Time: 20 mins | Cook Time: 40 minutes | Total Time: 1 hour

Serves: 4

Ingredients for Puttu:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt to taste

Instructions for Puttu:

  1. In a bowl, mix rice flour and salt. Sprinkle water little by little and mix until the flour resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Layer the puttu maker with grated coconut and the prepared rice flour mixture alternatively.
  3. Steam for about 10-15 minutes until the puttu is cooked and appears fluffy.

Ingredients for Kadala Curry:

  • 2 cups black chickpeas (kadala)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 3-4 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions for Kadala Curry:

  1. Soak the black chickpeas in water overnight. Drain and rinse.
  2. Pressure cook the chickpeas with enough water and salt until they are soft and cooked through.
  3. In a pan, heat coconut oil. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, garlic, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are mushy.
  6. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
  7. Add the cooked chickpeas along with its water. Mix well and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  8. Finally, add thick coconut milk and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  9. Adjust the seasoning and serve hot with puttu.

Pulihora

A tangy and spicy South Indian rice dish flavored with tamarind and spices.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 35 minutes



Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 lemon-sized tamarind ball
  • 3 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1 teaspoon urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Cook rice with 2 cups of water and let it cool down.
  2. Soak the tamarind in warm water for 10 minutes. Extract the juice and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add chana dal, urad dal, peanuts, cashew nuts (if using), dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until dals turn golden brown.
  5. Add turmeric powder and sauté for a few seconds.
  6. Add the tamarind extract, salt, and allow it to boil until the raw smell of tamarind disappears.
  7. Add the cooked rice and mix well until all the rice grains are coated with the tamarind mixture.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot or at room temperature.

Kerala Prawn Curry

A traditional Kerala-style curry featuring succulent prawns cooked in a flavorful coconut-based gravy.

Preparation Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 25 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes


Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500g prawns, cleaned and deveined
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a pan and add fenugreek seeds. Allow them to splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions, green chilies, and curry leaves. Saute until onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Mix well.
  6. Add cleaned prawns and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Pour thick coconut milk and water. Mix gently.
  8. Cover and cook on low heat for 10-15 minutes or until the prawns are cooked through.
  9. Adjust the consistency of the curry according to your preference by adding more water if needed.
  10. Once done, garnish with curry leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or appam.

Medhu Vada

A popular South Indian savory snack made from urad dal batter, deep-fried to golden perfection.

Preparation Time: 8 hours (for soaking dal) | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 8 hours 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (black lentils)
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • A few curry leaves, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Wash urad dal thoroughly and soak it in water for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked dal and grind it into a smooth batter, adding very little water if necessary.
  3. Add chopped green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, rice flour (if using), and salt to the batter. Mix well.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  5. Wet your hands, take a small portion of the batter, and shape it into a flat disc with a hole in the center.
  6. Gently slide the vada into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove the vadas from oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  8. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.
