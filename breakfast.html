Recipes & Cooking Ideas

Explore the diverse culinary delights of Tamil Nadu, Kerala, and Andhra Pradesh.

From the fragrant spices of Tamil Nadu to the coconut-infused dishes of Kerala and the fiery delicacies of Andhra Pradesh, discover the rich flavors of South India.

Dosa

Thin, crispy crepes made from fermented rice and lentil batter, typically served with sambar and coconut chutney.

Prep Time: 10-12 hours (includes soaking and fermenting time) | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 12 hours 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup parboiled rice (or idli rice)
  • 1/2 cup whole urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1/4 cup flattened rice (poha)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together in water. Soak them in enough water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Rinse the flattened rice (poha) and soak it separately in water for about 30 minutes.
  3. Drain the water from rice, dal, and fenugreek seeds. Grind them together into a smooth batter using a wet grinder or blender, adding water as needed.
  4. Add soaked flattened rice (poha) to the batter and grind again until well combined.
  5. Add salt to the batter and mix well. The batter should be of pouring consistency, similar to pancake batter.
  6. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours or overnight, allowing it to rise and become slightly sour.
  7. Heat a non-stick or cast iron griddle (tawa) over medium-high heat.
  8. Grease the griddle lightly with oil or ghee.
  9. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the center of the hot griddle and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  10. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa.
  11. Cook until the underside turns golden brown and crispy, then flip and cook the other side for a minute.
  12. Remove the dosa from the griddle and serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.

Idli

Steamed rice cakes made from fermented rice and lentil batter, typically served with sambar and coconut chutney.

Prep Time: 8-10 hours | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 10 hours 15 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)
  • 1 cup whole urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee (for greasing the idli molds)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the idli rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately and soak them in water for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked rice, dal, and fenugreek seeds. Grind them together in a wet grinder or blender to make a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Add salt to the batter and mix well. The batter should have a thick pouring consistency.
  4. Transfer the batter to a large bowl and cover it. Allow it to ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours or until the batter doubles in volume and becomes airy.
  5. Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee. Pour spoonfuls of the batter into each mold.
  6. Steam the idlis in a steamer for about 10-12 minutes or until they are cooked through and firm.
  7. Remove the idlis from the molds using a spoon or knife.
  8. Serve hot idlis with sambar and coconut chutney.

Pongal

A traditional South Indian dish made from rice and lentils, flavored with cumin and black pepper.

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 25 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/2 cup split yellow moong dal (yellow lentils)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns, crushed
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • Chopped coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Rinse rice and moong dal together under running water. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a pressure cooker or pot, heat ghee over medium heat.
  3. Add cumin seeds, crushed peppercorns, and asafoetida. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add curry leaves, chopped green chilies, and ginger. Sauté for another minute.
  5. Add the rinsed rice and moong dal to the pot. Mix well with the tempering.
  6. Add salt and water. Stir to combine.
  7. Cover the pressure cooker or pot with a lid. Cook on medium heat for about 3 whistles (if using a pressure cooker) or simmer until rice and dal are cooked and soft.
  8. Once done, open the lid carefully and mix the pongal well. Adjust salt if needed.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Adai

A thick and savory pancake made from a mixture of lentils and rice, usually served with aviyal or chutney.

Prep Time: 4-6 hours | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 6 hours 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1/2 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black lentils)
  • 2-3 dried red chilies
  • 1 inch piece of ginger, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, chopped
  • A handful of curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak rice, toor dal, chana dal, and urad dal together in water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them into a coarse batter along with dried red chilies, ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, asafoetida, cumin seeds, and salt. Add water as needed to achieve a thick pancake batter consistency.
  3. Heat a griddle or non-stick pan over medium heat.
  4. Grease the pan with a little oil.
  5. Take a ladleful of the batter and spread it evenly on the pan to form a thick pancake (adai).
  6. Drizzle a little oil around the edges and cook until the bottom side turns golden brown and crispy.
  7. Flip the adai and cook the other side until cooked through.
  8. Repeat with the remaining batter to make more adais.
  9. Serve hot adais with aviyal, coconut chutney, or jaggery.

Appam

Soft and fluffy pancakes made from fermented rice batter, usually served with coconut milk.

Prep Time: 8-10 hours (includes fermentation time) | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 8-10 hours 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups raw rice
  • 1 cup cooked rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil or ghee, for greasing the appam pan

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak raw rice in water for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. In a blender, combine soaked raw rice, cooked rice, grated coconut, sugar, and salt.
  3. Add water gradually and blend until you get a smooth batter (similar to dosa batter consistency).
  4. Dissolve yeast in a little warm water and add it to the batter. Mix well.
  5. Cover the batter and let it ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight in a warm place.
  6. After fermentation, the batter will rise and become slightly bubbly.
  7. Heat an appam pan (appachatti) over medium heat and grease it lightly with oil or ghee.
  8. Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan.
  9. Hold the handles of the pan and gently swirl it around to spread the batter in a circular shape (thin edges and thick center).
  10. Cover the pan with a lid and cook the appam for 2-3 minutes or until the edges turn golden brown and crispy, and the center is cooked and fluffy.
  11. Remove the appam from the pan using a spatula and repeat with the remaining batter.
  12. Serve hot with coconut milk or stew.

Idiyappam

String hoppers made from rice flour, typically served with coconut milk or curry.

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 35 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice flour
  • 1 to 1.5 cups hot water
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine rice flour and salt.
  2. Gradually add hot water, a little at a time, and mix with a spoon until the flour comes together to form a dough.
  3. Knead the dough with your hands until smooth and pliable. Add oil and knead again to incorporate.
  4. Fill an idiyappam maker (or a chakli press with idiyappam attachment) with the dough.
  5. Press the dough onto idli plates or steamer trays in circular motions to form string hoppers.
  6. Steam the idiyappam for about 8-10 minutes or until cooked and firm.
  7. Once done, remove the idiyappam from the steamer and let them cool slightly.
  8. Serve warm idiyappam with coconut milk and sugar, or with curry of your choice.

Poori

Deep-fried bread made from wheat flour, often served with potato masala.

Prep Time: 20 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 tablespoon semolina (sooji, optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil, for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour, semolina (if using), and salt.
  2. Add water gradually and knead to form a smooth and stiff dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
  4. Heat oil for deep frying in a pan over medium-high heat.
  5. Roll out each dough ball into a small circle using a rolling pin (about 4-5 inches in diameter).
  6. Gently slide the rolled dough into the hot oil. Press it lightly with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
  7. Fry until both sides are golden brown and puffed up, turning as needed.
  8. Remove the poori from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  9. Serve hot with potato masala or any curry of your choice.

Maggi Noodles

Quick and easy noodles dish, perfect for a snack or light meal.

Prep Time: 5 minutes | Cook Time: 5 minutes | Total Time: 10 minutes

Serves: 2

Ingredients:

  • 2 packets of Maggi noodles
  • 4 cups of water
  • Maggi masala (included in the packet)
  • Chopped vegetables (optional, such as carrots, peas, bell peppers)
  • Oil or butter for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Bring 4 cups of water to a boil in a pot.
  2. Add Maggi noodles and cook for 2 minutes.
  3. If using vegetables, add them to the boiling water along with the noodles.
  4. After 2 minutes, add Maggi masala to the noodles. Stir well.
  5. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the noodles are cooked through.
  6. Remove from heat and serve hot.

Kuzhi Paniyaram

Kuzhi Paniyaram is a popular South Indian snack made from fermented rice and lentil batter, cooked in a special pan to get a crispy exterior and soft interior.

Prep Time: 8-10 hours (including soaking and fermentation) | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 8 hours 20 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw rice (preferably idli rice)
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black lentils)
  • 1/4 cup cooked rice (leftover rice)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup grated coconut (optional)
  • Oil for greasing the paniyaram pan

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak raw rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together in water for 6-8 hours.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them into a smooth batter along with cooked rice and salt. The consistency should be similar to dosa batter.
  3. Transfer the batter to a bowl and let it ferment for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  4. After fermentation, the batter will rise and become slightly fluffy.
  5. Heat a kuzhi paniyaram pan and grease each mold with a few drops of oil.
  6. Add chopped onion, green chilies, coriander leaves, and grated coconut (if using) to the batter and mix well.
  7. Spoon the batter into each mold, filling them up to 3/4th of the mold.
  8. Cover and cook on medium-low heat for a few minutes.
  9. Once the bottom side turns golden brown and crispy, flip each paniyaram using a skewer or spoon.
  10. Cook the other side until it becomes golden brown and crispy.
  11. Remove the paniyaram from the pan and repeat the process with the remaining batter.
  12. Serve hot with coconut chutney or tomato chutney.
