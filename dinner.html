Parotta

Layered flatbread originating from South India, made from maida (all-purpose flour), typically served with curry or salna.

Prep Time: 30 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 50 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water, as needed

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour and salt.
  2. Add vegetable oil or ghee to the flour mixture and mix well until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add water, little by little, and knead into a soft and smooth dough. The dough should be elastic but not sticky.
  4. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll each ball into a thin, elongated shape using a rolling pin. Brush the rolled dough with oil or ghee.
  5. Starting from one end, make pleats in the rolled dough, then roll it into a tight spiral. Tuck the end underneath the spiral.
  6. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls.
  7. Heat a tawa (flat skillet) over medium heat. Place a rolled parotta on the hot tawa and cook until golden brown spots appear on both sides, brushing with oil or ghee as needed.
  8. Remove the parotta from the tawa and gently press along the edges to fluff it up.
  9. Serve hot with your favorite curry or salna.

Chapati

Soft and fluffy Indian flatbread made from whole wheat flour, perfect to serve with curries or as a wrap.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt, to taste
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well.
  2. Add water gradually and knead the flour to make a smooth, soft dough. The dough should be firm but pliable.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small equal-sized balls. Roll each ball into a smooth ball between your palms.
  5. Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your hands. Dust it with flour and roll it into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin.
  6. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium-high heat.
  7. Place the rolled chapati on the hot tawa and cook for about 30 seconds or until small bubbles start to appear on the surface.
  8. Flip the chapati and cook the other side for about 1 minute or until light brown spots appear.
  9. Brush the cooked chapati with oil or ghee and flip again. Press gently with a spatula to puff up the chapati.
  10. Once fully cooked and lightly browned on both sides, remove the chapati from the tawa and place it in a hot case or wrap it in a clean kitchen towel to keep it warm.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining dough balls to make more chapatis.
  12. Serve hot with your favorite curry or vegetable dish.

Egg Curry

A flavorful dish made with boiled eggs cooked in a rich and spicy tomato-based gravy, perfect to pair with rice or bread.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 6 boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add water to adjust the consistency of the gravy and bring it to a simmer.
  8. Add boiled eggs to the gravy and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  9. Sprinkle garam masala and chopped coriander leaves on top.
  10. Turn off the heat and let it sit for a few minutes before serving.
  11. Serve hot with rice or bread.

Paneer Masala

A delicious and creamy Indian curry made with paneer (Indian cottage cheese) cooked in a rich tomato-based gravy.

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 20 minutes | Total Time: 35 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Now, add tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a minute.
  7. Add cubed paneer and mix gently to coat the paneer with the masala.
  8. Finally, add fresh cream and stir well.
  9. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, then turn off the heat.
  10. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Vegetable Noodles

A flavorful and colorful dish made with stir-fried noodles, mixed vegetables, and savory sauces.

Prep Time: 15 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 200 grams noodles (any type you prefer)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup cabbage, thinly sliced
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped spring onions (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  3. Add minced garlic and sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add sliced onions and green chilies (if using). Sauté until onions turn translucent.
  5. Add julienned carrots, sliced bell peppers, and sliced cabbage. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.
  6. In a small bowl, mix together soy sauce, chili sauce, vinegar, sugar, and salt.
  7. Add the cooked noodles to the pan and pour the sauce mixture over them. Toss well to coat the noodles and vegetables evenly with the sauce.
  8. Cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously, until everything is heated through.
  9. Turn off the heat and garnish with chopped spring onions.
  10. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious vegetable noodles!

Mushroom Pepper Fry

A delicious and spicy dish made with mushrooms and freshly ground black pepper, perfect as a side dish or appetizer.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 15 minutes | Total Time: 25 minutes

Serves: 4

Ingredients:

  • 500g mushrooms, cleaned and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns, crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add sliced onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add sliced mushrooms and cook until they release moisture and become tender.
  5. Add crushed black peppercorns, turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Cook for another 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms are coated with the spices and cooked through.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
